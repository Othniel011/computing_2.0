MILD OFFENSES

Mild offenses from the first to the fourth instance are handled by the subject teacher or homeroom teacher.
Starting from the fifth offense, the matter is handled by the Student Affairs department.

Violation no and consequences
  • 1-2: Verbal warning and issuance of a red offense slip by the teacher/staff.
  • 3: Verbal warning,red offense slip, and assignment of community service by the homeroom teacher.
  • 4-5: Verbal warning and red offense slip by teacher/staff.
  • 6: Verbal warning,red offense slip, community service, and parental summons.
  • 7-8: Verbal warning and red offense slip.
  • 9: Verbal warning,red offense slip, and community service.
  • 10-11: Verbal warning and red offense slip.
  • 12-17: Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parentsummons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs for the issuance of Warning Letter 1.
  • 18-21: Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parentsummons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs for the issuance of Warning Letter 2.
  • 22: Verbal warning, red violation slips, community service,parentsummons, and referral to the Vice Principal of Student Affairsfor the issuance of Warning Letter 3.
Mentoring
  1. Regularreporting to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs
  2. Mentoring by the homeroomteacher/mentor
  3. Counselling by the school counsellor

MODERATE OFFENSES

The first instance of a moderate offense is handled by the subject or homeroom teacher.
From the second offense onward, it is managed directly by Student Affairs.

Violation no, consequences & Mechanism
  • 1: Verbal warning.
  • Mechanism: Teachers/staff report the violation via an offense slip to Student Affairs.
  • 2: notification letter and agreement letter
  • Mechanism: Follow-up actions are handled by the Student Affairs department.
  • 3: Parental summons and issuance of a second formal warning letter (maximum).
  • Additional Consequences: Deduction of discipline score on the report card
  • Mechanism: Assigned tasks in the library and dismissal at 16:00 for 10 active school days
Mentoring
  1. Regularreporting to the Vice Principal of Student Affairs
  2. Mentoring by the homeroomteacher/mentor
  3. Counselling by the school counsellor

SEVERE OFFENSES

Severe offenses are handled directly by the Student Affairs departmentstarting from the first occurrence and so on.

Consequence and Mechanism
  • Violation no.1: Issuance of a third and final warning letter and the student is returned to their parents.
  • Mechanism: The violation is reported using an offense slip by any teacher/staff member to Student Affairs. Follow-up actions are handled by the Student Affairs department.
